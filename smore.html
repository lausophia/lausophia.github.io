02 | case study
Our venture zeroed in on researching and identifying the primary difficulties that immigrants and international students face when trying to culturally integrate into American society.

This focus stemmed from our team's common interest in languages, particularly the ways in which they may serve as barriers for foreigners to feel "at home" in a country other than their own, even if they have been studying or working there for several years.

03 | design
Name & Logo
The s'more is something that the typical, American born person would recognize immediately, having grown up either making it themselves, seen it in media, or having been exposed through friends. However, it is also something so niche to American culture, that it may be initially surprising to think that people from different backgrounds would have no idea what it is, let alone its role in American culture.

For this reason, we thought that it would be the perfect way to both encapsulate and differentiate our product's vision of facilitating the immersion experience.

UI/UX
For the product's UI/UX, I prioritized minimalism, taking much inspiration from interfaces that the average user would already most likely be familiar with, such as Pinterest's home page, Instagram's explore feed, Twitter's news highlights, and even UberEats' cuisine options.

We wanted to ensure that, due to this application's novelty, the user would need minimal onboarding or context of the app's purpose to be able to jump in and try it for his or herself.
