CodeBase Website Rebrand | Graphic, Branding

The summer of 2018, I worked very closely with Cindy Yuan, the VP of Marketing of UC Berkeley's consulting club, CodeBase in an effort to completely rebrand the club's image. Our goal was to provide the club with a more professional appearance to both potential new members and clients for the upcoming year. This page describes the progress of CodeBase's summer rebrand and documents the thought process behind each design decision.
Home Page

Clarified the goals and values of the club immediately on the home page.

Replaced unclear "services" button with a direct link for students to apply.

Traded a playful, doodle-filled background with a solid color to maximize professionalism.

Used a bold visual number representation to highlight the most salient club aspects.

Projects Page

Centered body elements to maintain format consistency throughout website.

Included a more cohesive introduction to the project experiences offered each semester.

Used company logo as header for a more readable, visual presentation.

Replaced theme color with white to increase contrast and to have a cleaner appearance.

Utilized separate headings to explicitly distinguish the project category: client vs. mentored.

Project Structure

Replaced large chunk of text into a visual timeline of events.

Removed unrelated team photo.

Action Section

Placed related icons in header of each column.

Added description to clarify purpose of the page section for each respective type of audience.

Increased size of container to make the displayed links more likely to be utilized after people finish reading the project page.

Project Details

Project description segmented into more than two large sections.

Introduces the goals of the company/project before immediately describing the project itself.

Included the term during which the project was completed.

Application Page

Side-by-side comparison of two offered project teams students may join replaces uninformative direct links to application page.

Descriptions consist of short phrases instead of long sentences for better audience readability.

Maintained a clearer distinction between the project types based on the experience level and goals of the student applicant.

About Page

Split up large chunks of text into more readable subsections.

Portrayed club with a clear set of values rather than a personal description.

Visual icons used to represent each of the listed base behaviors.
