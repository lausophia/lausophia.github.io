CodeBase Website Rebrand | Graphic, Branding

The summer of 2018, I worked very closely with Cindy Yuan, the VP of Marketing of UC Berkeley's software development club, CodeBase in an effort to completely rebrand the club's image. Our goal was to provide the club with a more professional appearance to both potential new members and clients for the upcoming year. This page describes the progress of CodeBase's summer rebrand and documents the thought process behind each design decision.
Identifying Problems and Creating Goals

– Previous website failed to achieve its primary goal: informing two different audiences about what CodeBase does.

– Two audiences that need to be appealed to more precisely and directly:

1) aspiring software developers at UC Berkeley, and

2) Bay Area startup clients with projects in need of implementing.

– Goal for audience's perception of what CodeBase is: a student-based, on campus software-development organization that pairs teams of students with semester-long projects.

– Unclear description of the distinction between the two projects offered to students with different levels of experience.

Ideation and Prototyping

– Tried a more center-focused page structure.

– Incorporated more visuals to break up text-heavy paragraphs to improve website readability.

– Replaced playful background images with a more minimalistic theme to preserve professionalism.

– Used side-by-side graphics to emphasize distinctions between different project types, appeal to different audiences, etc.

Icon Design

– Used clean, thin lines to maintain consistency in professionalism.

- Uniform background patterns for a more playful edge to the otherwise simple icons.

- Dark blue consistent with website's theme color.

- Shades of pink used as an accent color to liven up the rest of the website, as well as CodeBase's recruitment fliers, and other promotional media.

Final Product
